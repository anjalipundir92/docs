Viithii's maintenance module gives you full visibility into every repair and service request across your portfolio. Whether a tenant reports a leaking faucet or an emergency HVAC failure, you can assign work to the right vendor, track progress in real time, and keep everyone informed automatically at each step.

## How Tenants Submit Requests

Tenants access the Viithii tenant portal using their login credentials. Once inside, they follow these steps to submit a maintenance request:

1. Click **Submit Request** in the portal navigation.
2. Enter a short title and a detailed description of the issue.
3. Attach one or more photos to help your team assess the problem before visiting.
4. Select an urgency level — **Low**, **Medium**, **High**, or **Emergency**.
5. Click **Submit** to send the request to your dashboard immediately.

<Note>
  Tenants receive an automatic confirmation email as soon as their request is submitted. Every subsequent status change also triggers an email notification, so tenants are never left wondering about the status of their repair.
</Note>

## Managing Requests

All incoming requests appear in the **Maintenance** section of your Viithii dashboard. Use the steps below to move a request from open to closed.

<Steps>
  <Step title="View open requests">
    Navigate to **Maintenance** in the left sidebar. The default view shows all **Open** requests sorted by urgency. Use the filters at the top to narrow by property, category, or date range.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to a vendor or team member">
    Open a request and click **Assign**. Select a vendor or an internal team member from the dropdown. If the person or company you need isn't listed yet, see **Adding Vendors** below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the request status">
    As work progresses, update the status to reflect current reality. Move the request through the status flow as needed:
    - **Open** → **In Progress** → **Pending Parts** → **Resolved** → **Closed**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark the request complete">
    Once the work is confirmed finished, set the status to **Closed**. Optionally add internal notes or attach invoice documentation before closing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Assign a **category** to every request — **Plumbing**, **Electrical**, **HVAC**, or **General** — to keep your maintenance history organized and make it easy to pull cost reports by category later.
</Tip>

## Adding Vendors

Before you can assign work orders to a contractor, you need to add them as a vendor in your account.

1. Go to **Settings** → **Vendors** and click **Add Vendor**.
2. Enter the vendor's name, contact email, phone number, and trade specialty.
3. Optionally upload their insurance certificate and license details for your records.
4. Click **Save**. The vendor now appears in the **Assign** dropdown on any maintenance request.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Can I create a maintenance request on behalf of a tenant?">
  Yes. As a property manager, you can log a request directly from your dashboard without requiring tenant action. Go to **Maintenance** → **New Request**, select the unit and tenant, fill in the issue details, and submit. This is especially useful when a tenant reports a problem by phone or in person. The request enters the same workflow and the tenant can be notified automatically if you choose.
</Accordion>
