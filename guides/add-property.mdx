Viithii organizes everything — tenants, leases, maintenance, and payments — around your properties. Before you can onboard tenants or collect rent, you need to add at least one property and configure its units. This guide walks you through creating a property, building out its unit structure, and maintaining your portfolio over time.

## Adding a Property

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Properties section">
    Click **Properties** in the left sidebar. If this is your first property, you'll see an empty state with a prompt to get started.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new property">
    Click **Add Property** in the top-right corner of the Properties page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter property details">
    Fill in the required fields:
    - **Property Name** — a friendly label (e.g., "Maple Street Duplex")
    - **Address** — street, city, state, and ZIP code
    - **Property Type** — choose **Single-Family**, **Multi-Family**, or **Commercial**

    These details appear on leases, tenant statements, and maintenance requests, so make sure they're accurate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload property photos (optional)">
    Click **Upload Photos** to add images of the property exterior or common areas. Photos help you and your team identify properties at a glance inside the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the property">
    Click **Save Property**. Viithii creates the property record and takes you to the property detail page, where you can start adding units.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding Units to a Property

Every rentable space inside a property is represented as a unit. For a single-family home, you'll typically have one unit. Multi-family and commercial properties can have as many units as needed.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the property">
    From the **Properties** list, click the property you just created to open its detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a unit">
    Click **Add Unit**. A form panel slides open on the right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter unit details">
    Provide the following information:
    - **Unit Number** — e.g., "1A", "Unit 3", or "Suite 200"
    - **Bedrooms** and **Bathrooms** — numeric values
    - **Square Footage**
    - **Rent Amount** — the monthly asking rent for this unit
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the unit">
    Click **Save**. The unit appears in the property's unit list and is now available to assign to a tenant.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing Property Details

You can update a property's name, address, type, or photos at any time. Open the property detail page, click **Edit Property** in the top-right corner, make your changes, and click **Save Property**. Changes take effect immediately and are reflected across all associated records, including lease documents.

## Archiving a Property

If you no longer manage a property, you can remove it from your active portfolio by archiving it. Open the property detail page, click the **⋯ More** menu, and select **Archive Property**.

<Warning>
  Archiving a property hides it from your active **Properties** list and prevents new leases or tenants from being assigned to it. However, all historical records — leases, payments, maintenance requests, and tenant data — are fully preserved and remain accessible through search and reports.
</Warning>

To restore an archived property, go to **Properties** → **Archived**, find the property, and click **Restore**.

<Tip>
  If you're onboarding a large portfolio, you don't have to add properties one at a time. Use the **CSV Import** feature under **Properties** → **Import** to upload dozens of properties and their units in a single file. Download the CSV template from that page to see the required column format.
</Tip>
