Bringing a new tenant into Viithii connects their identity, lease, and payment activity to a specific unit in your portfolio. Once onboarded, tenants receive access to the Viithii Tenant Portal — a self-service hub where they can pay rent, submit maintenance requests, and view their lease documents. Follow the steps below to get a new tenant set up from start to finish.

## Onboarding Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Add Tenant form">
    Click **Tenants** in the left sidebar, then click **Add Tenant** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter tenant details">
    Fill in the tenant's personal information:
    - **Full Name**
    - **Email Address** — used to send the portal invitation
    - **Phone Number**
    - **Date of Birth**
    - **Government ID** — driver's license or passport number for identity verification

    All fields except **Government ID** are required to proceed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign the tenant to a unit">
    Under **Unit Assignment**, select the **Property** from the dropdown, then select the specific **Unit**. Only vacant units are shown. If you don't see the unit you need, make sure it has been added to the property first — see [Add and Manage Properties](/guides/add-property).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the move-in date">
    Enter the tenant's **Move-In Date**. This date is used to calculate prorated rent on the first invoice and marks the start of the tenancy in your records.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a lease">
    Click **Create Lease** to generate a lease agreement tied to this tenant and unit. You can use a Viithii lease template or upload your own. For full details on lease terms, signatures, and renewals, see the [Lease Management](/guides/lease-management) guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the portal invitation">
    Click **Send Portal Invitation**. Viithii sends the tenant an email with a link to create their Tenant Portal account. Once they sign up, they can immediately log in to view their lease, make payments, and submit maintenance requests.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The portal invitation link expires after **72 hours**. If a tenant misses the window, open their tenant record, go to the **Portal Access** tab, and click **Resend Invitation** to issue a fresh link.
</Note>

## Tenant Portal Access

The Viithii Tenant Portal gives tenants a dedicated space to manage their tenancy without needing to contact you directly. Once a tenant activates their account, they can:

- **Pay rent** via bank transfer (ACH) or debit/credit card, and set up autopay
- **Submit maintenance requests** with photos and priority levels, and track status updates
- **View and download documents** including their lease agreement, addenda, and payment receipts
- **Update contact information** such as phone number and emergency contacts

Encouraging tenants to use the portal reduces inbound calls and keeps all communication and payments in one auditable place.

## Co-Tenants

If multiple adults are sharing a unit — such as roommates or a couple — you can add each person as a co-tenant. Open the unit's tenant record, scroll to the **Co-Tenants** section, and click **Add Co-Tenant**. Enter the co-tenant's personal details using the same fields as the primary tenant.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Shared Lease" icon="file-signature" href="/guides/lease-management">
    All co-tenants are listed on the same lease agreement and are jointly responsible for rent.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Individual Portal Access" icon="user" href="/guides/onboard-tenant#tenant-portal-access">
    Each co-tenant receives their own portal invitation and logs in with their own credentials.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Viithii integrates with leading background and credit check providers. Before finalizing a tenant, click **Run Background Check** on the tenant detail page to screen the applicant directly within the platform — no need to switch to a separate service. Results are stored on the tenant record for your records.
</Tip>
